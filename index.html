Welcome to my portfolio!

My name is Noah Ramirez. I am a copywriter and content specialist with over ten years of experience working in areas of localization, descriptive metadata, digital marketing, and business journalism. My professional expertise is in cross-functional communications, setting style and procedural standards, designing workflows, and coordinating multilingual content-driven projects for major global brands in Latin America. When I am not working to connect people and ideas or striving to get the most out of content (as well as the people and technologies that bring it to life), I enjoy running, playing soccer on the weekend, reading, shanking golf balls, badly managing fantasy football teams, e-learning and practicing coding, binge-listening true crime podcasts, and spending time with my beautiful family.

Roles

International Content Coordinator

  • The person who makes multilingual content projects “Go”. I work to bring new content projects to life and ensure operational success once they’re implemented. This professional experience has given valuable insight into developing workflows, establishing writing and editing processes and standards, managing, storing and analyzing data derived from content metrics, and client communications.

Style Editor

  • As an editor and advisor, I guide a team of multilingual copywriters, translators and journalists to ensure that every work of content has been prepared with style, tone, accuracy, and target audience in mind. This often involves staying up to date with new terminology and technologies, SEO writing, facilitating cross-functional collaboration, and drafting and maintaining style guides.

Marketing QA Specialist

  • I am dotting the i's and crossing the t's when it comes to digital marketing. Being a QA marketing specialist, I observe marketing campaigns from their inception to their delivery, which requires shaping and adapting the message on the creative side, and then making sure that the message and any supporting assets (images, videos, links, calendars) arrive functional and fully optimized according to their delivery method (be it by email, push, In-App, etc.)
